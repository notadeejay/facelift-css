Category Key Goal Questions You Might Ask Important Notes
Business Process Goals Identify the goals of the business process, both for the business and the people involved with/affected by the process.
  • What is the objective of this process?
  • What do you use this process for?
  • What is your role in this process?
Keep in mind that goals will sometimes vary across stakeholders, depending on their individual involvement with the business process. However, the overarching goal of the process for the business at large will likely remain the same.
As-Is Process Steps Develop a complete understanding of the as-is business process and document it.
  • What causes this process to begin? What is the first step in the process?
  • When do you know this process is finished? What is the ending point?
  • What are the steps involved in this process?
  • Why is the process done this way?
The process steps you hear might differ among different people you interview, especially when considering process steps that are external to Salesforce.
Pain Points & Issues Surface core pain points and potential issues or gaps with the existing process.
  • What is your #1 pain point with this process? 
  • What would you say is the biggest area for improvement?
  • What is the #1 complaint partners (or customers) have?
You should independently investigate and validate pain points and potential issues that interviewees surface during interviews before regarding them as genuine issues. You should also keep the larger goal(s) of the business process in mind when considering which pain points are important to address.
Potential Improvements Determine possible improvements for a to-be process.
  • If you could design the ideal process, what would that look like?
  • How could this step be simplified?
Suggestions from interviewees are not always the same as the actual best improvements to make to a process. Again, you should validate these yourself as a business analyst and keep the process goals in mind.
